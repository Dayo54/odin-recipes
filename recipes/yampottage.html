Description

Yam Pottage also known Asaro needs no major introduction. It is a very popular Nigerian mashed Yam and Tomato stew infused dish.

Ingridents

  • 1 tuber of yam chooped into small chunks
  • 2 cooking spoons of palm oil
  • 1 cup of washed and chooped spinach leaves
  • 2 red bell pepper
  • onions
  • 2 scootch bonnet
  • smoked turkey
  • ponmo
  • 1 medium sized stocked fish
  • locust beans
  • maggi
  • salt

steps

  1. Wash and boil the meats you’ll be using if you’re using any. Remember to boil, the tougher meats first, then add the softer meats along with the stockfish (Panla). If you’ll be using smoked fish, add this around 2-3 minuts before final doneness to soften it up. Do season them really well as you’ll be using some of the stock later.
  2. Shred the meats and fish into bite sizes and set aside.
  3. Get a fairly big pot, place on medium heat, add the palm oil. When it’s hot, add 1/2 sliced onions, sauté till fragrant
  4. After 2 minutes, add the blended pepper, bouillon cube and salt to taste. Cover and fry for 15 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent burning
  5. While the stew is frying, transfer the yam into a pot, add enough water to cover it, add just a bit of salt and parboil for 6-8 minutes. Don’t overcook or cook till soft, just till it’s a tad bit tender…
  6. Check on the stew, when it’s fried, you’ll know when the pepper reduces considerably and you can’t taste the sourness of the tomatoes
  7. Add 1-2 cups of stock, combine and leave to cook for a further 2-3 minutes
  8. Now, add the parboiled yam, along with the shredded meats
  9. Combine thoroughly, turn the heat down to low, cover and leave to cook for 15 minutes or until the yam softens.
  10. When the yam softens, mash half of the yam and cook for 5 minutes
  11. After 5 minutes, add the Ugu or whichever vegetable leaves you’re using…
  12. Switch off the heat at this time and let the vegetables simmer in the pottage with the residual heat for 2-3 minutes.
  13. And your Asaro – Yam Pottage is ready.
